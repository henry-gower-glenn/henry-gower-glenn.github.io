Bootcamp - Learning Plan - Sprint 5

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goal is to complete Dev Academy and then find a job as a junior front end developer. From there I would like to work my way into the back end and go from there. The company I would love to work for is Zip I would love to work for a startup company rather than an established company and I think BNPL companies are the next big thing.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My biggest strength will be my determination to complete this course. My biggest limitation will be my learning disadvantages and making sure my mentality does not feel like I am being left behind or am terrible at coding. I felt a bit like this during foundations.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Asking questions when necassary, I feel like after seeing people post in the slack channel during bootcamp that I am miles behind them in skill level and so I dont want to feel like I am stupid for asking potentially silly questions when everyone else understands whats going on. Although I thought having access to slack channel was an advatage, it can also play on your mind about how far behind you are compared to others.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

better time management. Sometimes I can get lost down rabbit holes and its nice to constatly have time reminders on how much time I should be investing into a particular exercise, even though I think some of the times provided were unrealistic.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

For teachers to answer all questions asked. For Dev Academy to ensure I am learning at a decent speed and I am not being left behind. To make studying life less stressful where possible. To constantly check-up and ask where I am up to and how I am going. 1 on 1 tutoring if required to ensure I pass this course.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

To ensure I ask for help in a timely manner by asking the slack channel, facilitators, teachers, friends and google. I will ensure I am investing a minimum of 60 hours a week and will try my best at completing the work that is provided to me in the most timely manner possible. I will make sure I get up early and have exercised and eating properly to ensure my body is always ready for learning. I will be considerate towards others and will try answer other peoples queries when possible.

Learning Plan - Sprint 2

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goal is to complete Dev Academy and then find a job as a junior front end developer. From there I would like to work my way into the back end and go from there.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning

I am very determined to complete this goal. I have been out of the workforce for 3 years now due to sickness so I am very eager to get back out there and start working. I am very honest with myself and know when I’m getting off path and to ‘pull my head in’. I am very logical and coding is all about logic so I’m sure I can use this to my advantage. I have ADHD which means my attention can be all over the place. I also have dyspraxia which means I ‘take a bit longer to absorb information’ than others.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

My communication skills. I am requiring a lot of help but find it hard to seek it and have the confidence to post my possible ‘silly’ questions in the foundations slack channel. So I would like to try and build up my confidence and communication skills.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include: An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

I try and use Toggl as best as I can to keep track off my time to ensure I at least put in 8-10 hour days. I make sure I exercise and eat properly every day to ensure my mind and body is in ‘working mode’. I try my best to keep to the recommended time targets even though I think some of them are unrealistic. I try and take regular small breaks if workloads are getting to me to ensure I’m always in the best frame of mind. I will seek help where needed to try and get myself out of potential holes. I will always use Google where I can to help out with sticky situations.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I will always try and google it myself to see if I can solve it. I will then seek help in the Foundations and cohort chat if I can still not solve it. If the problem keeps persisting and I don’t have a resolution I will then ask a Foundations facilitator.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

I expect the facilitation team to answer my queries where needed. To follow up on where I am at as a student and to make sure I have got the correct resources to complete the course.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It’s important to plan these in.
