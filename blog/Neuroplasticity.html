Discuss your understanding about Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset and how it influences your learning

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Understanding how Neuroplasticity works is beneficial because it gives people motivation to grow their brain. If people understand that they can grow their brain and become smarter rather than having fixed thinking then they are a lot more motivated to make that happen for themselves.

Discuss how you might engage with the principals of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

I personally have learning difficulties and had depression at one point and the fact you can use Neuroplasticity to help mitigate this blow my mine. I will constantly tell myself I can succeed in things and will seek out new ways to continuously grow my brain from now on.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity

1. Think about the action you are wanting to grow in. Mentality is key!
2. Practice, practice, practice, constantly make sure you practice and review things you have gone over before to solidify the connections in your brain.
3. Understanding that your brain is malleable increases the drive you have for growing it.

Discuss what Growth Mindset is and why it is relevant

Growth Mindset is a particular way of thinking. It’s being open to the fact you can grow and shape your brain and understand that your brain is not fixed. It is relevant because a lot of students these days get put into a belief of thinking that they are born with the intelligence they have and that they can’t increase their brain potential with a growth mindset. This restricts their learning and means they are focusing more on not looking dumb rather than wanting increase their knowledge with a growth mindset.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

My mother always talked about this mindset when I was a kid but I never paid attention. After seeing the evidence and the research I just did. I am going to be a lot more mindful and operate in a mindset of growth rather than fixed thinking. I always believed you were born with the potential you had I didn’t realise how powerful a growth mindset was. It also blew my mind that it can help with mitigating learning difficulties and depression.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

The biggest step is understanding that your brain is malleable and it’s not locked. I will constantly tell myself I can achieve things rather than saying I can’t.
